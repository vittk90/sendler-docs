# Running a Lab — I run a lab

Turn your collection into a crafting economy. Four steps:

## 1. Create a lab

One on-chain transaction:

* **Name:** 1–128 characters, Latin only
* **Creation fee:** 5 Ⓝ — any excess is refunded
* The lab starts empty; you add recipes and stock rewards from the lab page

## 2. Build a recipe

A recipe is just a trade you define: **what a crafter must give you, and what they get back.**

* **What they give (ingredients):** you can require a specific item, or set up a flexible **"any one of"** group — for example, accepting any single NFT from a big collection instead of one specific token.
* **Group whole NFT collections into a single ingredient** — this lets you accept any of thousands of tokens as valid payment, instead of listing them one by one.
* **What they get (the reward):** what a successful craft delivers.

## 3. Stock the reward

Transfer the reward FTs/NFTs into the lab. The recipe card automatically counts **how many crafts the current stock covers** — crafters always see real availability before they commit.

## 4. Activate

Flip the recipe live. Then track everything in **Insights**: crafts, crafters, and activity over time.

## Lab presentation

Your lab is your storefront. Customize it (off-chain, changes appear in seconds):

* **Logo** and **banner** (stored on IPFS)
* **Description** and **tags** (Latin only)
* Links: **X**, **Telegram**, **Reddit**, **Website**

## Danger Zone

**Delete Lab** is a *soft delete*: recipes stop accepting deposits and crafts, but **withdrawals stay open** — no one who deposited into your recipes is ever left stuck.

Platform limits apply to labs and recipes (shown as e.g. *Labs 3/5, Recipes 1/2* in Explore).
